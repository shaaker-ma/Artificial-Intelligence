Part 3-3:¶

Here is 6 solutions for missing data:

1- Do Nothing:
    well this is the most easy thing there is to do, and obviously we are not going to use it compare to the average
    replacement

2- Imputation Using (Mean/Median) Values:
    Our method above.

    -Disadvantages:
        -Mean imputation does not preserve the relationships among variables.
        -Mean Imputation Leads to An Underestimate of Standard Errors

    -Advantages:
        -Super easy

3- Imputation Using Most Frequent or Zero/Constant Values: 
    Most Frequent is another statistical strategy to impute missing values 

   - Benifits:
        -Works well with categorical features.

   - Consequences:
        -It does NOT factor the correlations between features
        - can introduce bias in the data.

4-Imputation Using k-NN: -> Better than 3 above
    KNN or k nearest neighbours is used for simple classification, it uses feature similarity to predict the values
    of any new data points.This means that the new point is assigned a value based on how closely it resembles the points
    in the training set.

    - Benifits:
        -Much more accurate than the mean, median or most frequent imputation methods.
